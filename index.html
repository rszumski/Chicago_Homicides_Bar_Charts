From 2001 to 2017 48.6 percent of the 8913 homicides in Chicago resulted in an arrest. To use the FBI's terminology, the national 'clearance rate' for homicide today is 64.1 percent. Fifty years ago, it was more than 90 percent.

The number of homicides has spiked in recent years while the arrest rate decreased. There have been 4609 homicides without an arrest between 2001 and 2017.
